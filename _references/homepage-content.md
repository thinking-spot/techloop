## Key Copywriting Techniques Used

### 1. Problem-First Framing
"Want to try smart glasses? That's $399 upfront. Hate them after a week? You're out $399."

This establishes the pain point immediately before offering solution.

### 2. Concrete Math
"Try 3 devices for $288 total. If you bought all 3 on Amazon to compare, that's $1,197. You save $909."

Specific numbers are more persuasive than vague "save money" claims.

### 3. Real User Language
"Look normal. Act superhuman." (Meta Ray-Ban)  
"Your body's story, told by a ring." (Oura)

Emotional, aspirational copy instead of just listing specs.

### 4. Objection Pre-emption
Every major objection addressed on homepage:
- "Are they new?" ‚Üí Yes, factory sealed
- "What if I damage it?" ‚Üí 98% get full deposit back
- "Can I cancel?" ‚Üí Yes, anytime, no fees

### 5. Choice Abundance
"Most people try 3-5 devices before finding 'the one.' Some stay forever renters. Some buy everything. There's no right path. Just your path."

Removes pressure, makes exploration feel okay.

### 6. Social Proof Specificity
Not just "thousands of users" but:
- 1,000+ users (specific number)
- 4.8/5 stars (347 reviews) (credible, not "perfect")
- 98% get full deposit back (shows we're not trying to scam)

### 7. Comparison Anchoring
"$48/month" feels cheap when anchored against "$399 upfront"

### 8. Future Pacing
"Work on a massive display from coffee shops. Watch movies on flights."

Helps reader visualize themselves using device.

### 9. Category Signal
Device cards have clear badges: SMART GLASSES, SMART RING, AI EARBUDS

Helps scanners find what they want quickly.

### 10. Micro-Commitments
First CTA is "Find Your Device" (quiz, low commitment)  
Not "Sign Up Now" (high commitment)
